## The Acropolis

The **Acropolis** (ἀκρόπολις, "high city") has been a site of worship since prehistoric times. The monuments visible today were largely built under **Pericles** in the 5th century BCE:

- **Parthenon** (447–432 BCE) — The supreme achievement of the Doric order; originally housed the chryselephantine (gold and ivory) statue of Athena by Pheidias
- **Erechtheion** (421–406 BCE) — Ionic temple housing ancient cult objects; famous Caryatid Porch
- **Propylaea** (437–432 BCE) — Grand entrance gateway
- **Temple of Athena Nike** (c. 420 BCE) — Small Ionic temple celebrating victory

## The Acropolis Museum

The modern **Acropolis Museum** (opened 2009) houses the original sculptures from the Acropolis, including the Caryatids, archaic korai, and pediment sculptures — displayed with views of the Parthenon above.

## The Ancient Agora

Below the Acropolis, the **Agora** was the civic center where Socrates debated, democratic assemblies prepared, and commerce thrived. Key remains include the reconstructed **Stoa of Attalos** (now a museum) and the remarkably well-preserved **Temple of Hephaestus**.
