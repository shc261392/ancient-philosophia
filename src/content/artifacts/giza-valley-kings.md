## Giza Plateau

The **Giza Necropolis** (c. 2580–2510 BCE) contains:
- **Great Pyramid of Khufu** — Originally 146m tall; the largest pyramid ever built and the last surviving Wonder of the Ancient World
- **Pyramid of Khafre** — Appears taller due to elevated position; retains limestone casing at the apex
- **Pyramid of Menkaure** — The smallest of the three main pyramids
- **Great Sphinx** — 73m-long limestone statue with the body of a lion and head of a human (probably Khafre)
- **Workers' village and bakeries** — Proving the pyramids were built by organized labor, not slaves

## Valley of the Kings (Thebes West)

The **Valley of the Kings** (c. 1539–1075 BCE) in West Thebes contains 63+ tombs of New Kingdom pharaohs:
- **KV62 (Tutankhamun)** — Discovered by Howard Carter in 1922 with treasures intact
- **KV17 (Seti I)** — The longest and most beautifully decorated royal tomb
- **KV9 (Ramesses V/VI)** — Stunning astronomical ceiling
