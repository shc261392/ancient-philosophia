## About the Collection

The Egyptian Museum (Tahrir Square building) has housed the world's preeminent collection of ancient Egyptian artifacts since 1902. While many treasures are being transferred to the **Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM)** at Giza, both facilities remain essential:

- **Tutankhamun galleries** — The golden mask, canopic jars, throne, chariots, and thousands of objects from the intact tomb
- **Old Kingdom sculpture** — Seated statues of Khafre, Rahotep and Nofret, and the wooden Ka-Aper
- **Middle Kingdom models** — Armies, workshops, and boats from the tomb of Mesehti
- **Papyrus collection** — Book of the Dead scrolls, mathematical papyri, and literary texts

## Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM)

The new Grand Egyptian Museum near the Giza pyramids is the world's largest archaeological museum. It will house over 100,000 artifacts, with the complete Tutankhamun collection displayed together for the first time.
