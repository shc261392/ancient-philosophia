## About the Collection

The Pergamon Museum (part of Berlin's Museum Island, a UNESCO World Heritage Site) contains three major collections:

- **Antikensammlung** — Greek and Roman antiquities, including the Pergamon Altar (the monumental Hellenistic sculptural frieze depicting the battle of the gods and giants) and the Market Gate of Miletus
- **Vorderasiatisches Museum** — Ancient Near Eastern art, crowned by the stunning **Ishtar Gate** of Babylon, reconstructed from original glazed bricks excavated by Robert Koldewey (1899–1917)
- **Museum für Islamische Kunst** — Islamic art including the Mshatta Facade

## The Ishtar Gate

The Ishtar Gate (c. 575 BCE), built by Nebuchadnezzar II, is one of the most spectacular ancient monuments in any museum. The brilliant blue-glazed bricks with relief animals (bulls and dragons/mušḫuššu) represent the pinnacle of Neo-Babylonian art. The Processional Way leading to the gate features striding lions.
