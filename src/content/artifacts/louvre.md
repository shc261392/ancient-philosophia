## About the Collection

The Louvre's ancient collections occupy multiple wings:

- **Egyptian Antiquities** — One of the world's largest Egyptological collections, curated since Champollion. Highlights include the crypt of Osiris, the Great Sphinx of Tanis, and the Seated Scribe.
- **Near Eastern Antiquities** — The Mesopotamian collection includes the Code of Hammurabi, Gudea statues, reliefs from Dur-Sharrukin (Khorsabad), and the Stele of the Vultures.
- **Greek, Etruscan, and Roman Antiquities** — The Venus de Milo, Winged Victory, Borghese Gladiator, and the spectacular Sarcophagus of the Spouses from Cerveteri.

## Online Collection

The Louvre's online **Collections** database provides images and information for over 480,000 works. Most images are available for non-commercial use.
