## About the Collection

The Oriental Institute (OI) at the University of Chicago is one of the world's premier centers for ancient Near Eastern studies. Its museum houses objects from the OI's own excavations across the Middle East:

- **Mesopotamian Gallery** — The colossal human-headed winged bull (lamassu) from Khorsabad, cuneiform tablets, cylinder seals, and Sumerian sculpture
- **Egyptian Gallery** — Objects from OI excavations at Medinet Habu, including a monumental statue of Tutankhamun
- **Persian Gallery** — Reliefs and architectural elements from Persepolis, brought from the OI's 1930s excavations
- **Syro-Anatolian Gallery** — Ivories from Megiddo, Tell Tayinat reliefs, and seals from the Amuq

## Research Projects

The OI produces many of the field's most important reference works:
- **Chicago Assyrian Dictionary (CAD)** — 21 volumes, completed 2011
- **Chicago Hittite Dictionary (CHD)** — Ongoing
- **Demotic Dictionary** — For late-period Egyptian
- **ORACC and ePSD2** — Digital cuneiform projects hosted by OI affiliates
