## About the Collection

The Penn Museum is one of the most important archaeological research museums in the world. Its collections derive largely from the university's own excavations:

- **Mesopotamian Collection** — Objects from Sir Leonard Woolley's excavations at Ur (1922–1934), including the spectacular gold and lapis lazuli treasures of the Royal Cemetery, and cuneiform tablets from Nippur
- **Egyptian Section** — Artifacts from Penn excavations at Memphis, Abydos, and other sites; includes the 13-ton sphinx of Ramesses II
- **Mediterranean Section** — Greek pottery, Roman glass, and Etruscan bronzes

## For Cuneiform Students

The Penn Museum houses one of the world's largest collections of cuneiform tablets. Many are being digitized as part of the **CDLI** project and annotated through **ORACC/ePSD2**.
