## About the Collection

The National Archaeological Museum of Athens houses the world's richest collection of ancient Greek art. Its holdings span from prehistory to late antiquity:

- **Prehistoric Collection** — Cycladic marble figurines, Mycenaean gold (including the Shaft Graves treasures from Mycenae), and Thera/Akrotiri frescoes
- **Sculpture Collection** — Kouroi and korai, Classical masterpieces, and Hellenistic works
- **Bronze Collection** — Including the Artemision Bronze and the Antikythera Youth
- **Vase and Minor Objects Collection** — Geometric, black-figure, and red-figure pottery
- **Egyptian and Near Eastern Collection** — Small but significant

## Highlights for Students of Linear B

The museum houses actual Linear B tablets from Mycenae and Pylos, allowing visitors to see the physical evidence of Mycenaean Greek administration — clay tablets preserved by accidental firing when the palaces burned.
