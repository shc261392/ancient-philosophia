## About the Collection

The Met's ancient world collections span three major departments:

- **Egyptian Art** — One of the finest Egyptian collections outside Cairo, with over 26,000 objects from the Paleolithic to the Roman period. Highlights include the Temple of Dendur, models from the tomb of Meketre, and Hatshepsut sculptures.
- **Greek and Roman Art** — Over 17,000 works from the Neolithic era through 312 CE. Outstanding vase collection, Cypriot sculpture, and Roman portrait busts.
- **Ancient Near Eastern Art** — Over 7,000 works from Mesopotamia, Iran, Anatolia, and Central Asia. Lamassu reliefs, cylinder seals, and Achaemenid treasures.

## Open Access

The Met's **Open Access Initiative** makes images of public-domain artworks available under **Creative Commons Zero (CC0)**, meaning they can be freely used for any purpose. Over 492,000 images are available for download via the website and API.
